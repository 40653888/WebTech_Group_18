Classical Music

Pieces from 1600-1900

Welcome to the Classical period

The Common-practice period was the most significant peroid in classical music, serving as the foundation of most Western music theory.
It encompassed the Baroque, Classical, and Romantic eras, spanding roughly 300 years from 1600 to 1900.

Baroque (1600-1750)

The Baroque period focused on ornate style, dramatic contrasts, and the use of basso continuo, a continuous bass line.

Music became more complex than previous periods, with keyboard music played on the harpsichord, and the pipe organ growing in popularity.

Famous composers and musicians:
Johann Sebastian Bach, Handel, and Vivaldi.

Classical (1750-1820)

The Classical era was the peroid from the 1750s to the early 1820s. During this, the Piano emerged as the most popular instrument, replacing the harpsichord.

The period focused on clarity, balance, and structure. Music became more refined and elegant, with simpler melodies and clearer forms like sonata form and symphonies.

Famous composers and musicians:
Ludwig van Beethoven, Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart, and Joseph Haydn.

Romantic (1820-1900)

Romantic music is all about emotion, individuality, and increased attention to an extended melodic line. Composers used music to tell stories and express deep feelings.

Harmony became richer, and dynamics more dramatic. The size of the orchestra, which was typically around 40 in the Classical era, grew to be over 100.

Famous composers and musicians:
Chopin, Schumann, Brahms, and Tchaikovsky.

Modern influence

  • The Baroque made the violin family of stringed instruments take the form generally seen today.

  • The use of complex harmonies and counterpoint has inspired genres like progressive rock and film scores.

  • Basso Continuo – The Baroque technique of a continuous bass line is echoed in basslines and rhythm sections in pop and jazz.
  • Classical made the basic forces required for an orchestra somewhat standarised.

  • The Classical era's focus on form and clarity shapes how modern pop and film music are structured.

  • Piano Fortepiano – The piano, refined in the Classical era, is the go-to for ballads, pop songwriting, and even lo-fi hip hop.
  • Romantic music’s emotional depth and storytelling continue in soundtracks, ballads, and even metal.

  • Full Orchestra – Romantic composers expanded the orchestra, and that large-scale sound continues in film and game scoring.
